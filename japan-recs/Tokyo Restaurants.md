# Tokyo Restaurant Recommendations

|------|-----------|-------|---------|------|------|--------------|--------|-------|
| Yoroniku (蕃 YORONIKU) | Silk Rose (thin-sliced wagyu wrapped around rice), Truffle Sukiyaki | Splurge ~13,000-21,500/person | 6-6-22 Minami-Aoyama, Luna Rossa B1F, Minato-ku, Tokyo | [Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Yoroniku+Minami+Aoyama+Tokyo) | [Tabelog](https://tabelog.com/en/tokyo/A1306/A130602/13042979/) | Yes - book via TableCheck or OMAKASE; cancel policy: auto-cancelled if 15min late | | Tabelog Top 100 2025 (score 4.16). Truffle Sukiyaki is the must-order. Also has branches in Ebisu and Azabudai Hills (private rooms). Innovative fusion of Japanese and Western grilling techniques. Multiple sources: Reddit r/JapanTravel, Tabelog, TripAdvisor, SAVOR JAPAN. |

Tabelog Gold 2017, Bronze 2025. Official Kobe Beef designated restaurant since 2023. Called "one of Japan's toughest reservations." The Straight Ball Course features 8 different premium cuts. Worth the trek to Asagaya. Wagyu paradise since 2011. Multiple sources: Tabelog, Reddit, food blogs. |

TripAdvisor 4.7/5, ranked #28 in Bunkyo. Considered one of only 5-6 yakiniku restaurants in Tokyo that "respect beef at the highest level." Menu is exclusively A5 Kuroge wagyu. Intimate atmosphere near Hongo Sanchome Station. Multiple sources: Tabelog, TripAdvisor, Reddit, food blogs. |
| Nikutei Futago iki Shinjuku (肉亭ふたご いき 新宿) | 80-dish wagyu menu with Ota beef (Hyogo) and Sendai beef (Miyagi) | Mid-range/Splurge ~5,000-15,000/person | 1-18-9 Kabukicho, WaMall 10F, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo | [Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nikutei+Futago+iki+Shinjuku+Tokyo) | [TripAdvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Restaurant_Review-g14133667-d12018022-Reviews-Nikutei_Futago_iki_Shinjuku-Kabukicho_Shinjuku_Tokyo_Tokyo_Prefecture_Kanto.html) | Recommended - private rooms available | | 10th-floor location in Kabukicho with elegant ambiance. The "Meat Box" (5,000 yen) is a popular starting point. Reviewers call it "the best wagyu BBQ experience." Good for groups with private/semi-private rooms. 6min walk from Shinjuku Station East Exit. More accessible than the reservation-impossible spots above. Multiple sources: TripAdvisor, byfood, food blogs. |

## Izakaya / Bars

| Name | Known For | Price | Address | Maps | Link | Reservations | Status | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|---------|------|------|--------------|--------|-------|
| Uokin Honten (魚金 本店) | 6-item sashimi platter, fresh seafood at wholesale prices | Budget ~2,000-4,000/person | Dai-2 Fuji Bldg B1-3F, 3-18-3 Shimbashi, Minato-ku, Tokyo | [Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Uokin+Honten+Shimbashi+Tokyo) | [Tabelog](https://tabelog.com/en/tokyo/A1301/A130103/13007107/) | No - walk-in; expect a queue after 6pm on weekdays | | Classic salaryman izakaya. Owned by a fish wholesale company so seafood prices are unbeatable -- "incoming price for other restaurants is selling price for Uokin." The signature 6-item sashimi platter is a must. Expect counter seating, noise, smokers -- that's the authentic charm. Open from 5pm weekdays, 4:30pm weekends. Quintessential Tokyo after-work energy. Multiple sources: Reddit, TripAdvisor, TimeOut, Tabelog. |
| Kanemasu (かねます) | Uniku (beef roll with sea urchin), standing izakaya with Tsukiji-fresh seafood | Mid-range ~5,000-6,000/person | Near Kachidoki Station, Chuo-ku, Tokyo | [Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kanemasu+Kachidoki+Tokyo) | [Tabelog](https://tabelog.com/en/tokyo/A1313/A131302/13002243/) | No - walk-in only; GO EARLY, best sellers sell out 30min after opening | | Famous standing izakaya near former Tsukiji market. The Uniku (uni + beef roll, 3,000 yen) is the signature dish everyone talks about. Sushi box (1,600 yen) also popular. Proximity to Tsukiji means unbeatable seafood freshness. Was packed at 3:30pm on a Tuesday -- that tells you everything. TimeOut Tokyo calls it one of the best izakayas in the city. Multiple sources: TimeOut Tokyo, Tabelog, Reddit, food blogs. |
| Ginza Shimada (銀座しまだ) | Kaiseki-quality standing bar, seasonal dishes at fraction of fine-dining prices | Mid-range ~3,000-6,000/person (courses up to 11,000) | 8-2-8 Ginza, Takasaka Bldg 1F, Chuo-ku, Tokyo (7min from JR Shimbashi) | [Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ginza+Shimada+Shimbashi+Tokyo) | [Tabelog](https://tabelog.com/en/tokyo/A1301/A130101/13136359/) | No reservations needed | | Tabelog Top 100 Tachinomi 2025 (score 3.66). Considered by many the best standing bar in Tokyo. Chef Hiroshi Shimada formerly worked at 3-Michelin-star kaiseki restaurant Azabu Yukimura -- he opened Shimada to offer the same quality to a broader audience at a fraction of the price. Dishes start at 500 yen. Chilled soba with karasumi (bottarga) or uni with spiny lobster are standout dishes. A pioneer that inspired the entire high-end tachinomi trend. Multiple sources: PUNCH, Tabelog, TimeOut Tokyo. |
| Birdland (鳥ランド) | Michelin-starred yakitori, Okukuji Shamo chicken, legendary liver pate | Mid-range/Splurge ~6,000-10,000/person | Ginza 4-2-15, Tsukamoto Sozan Bldg B1F, Chuo-ku, Tokyo | [Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Birdland+Ginza+Tokyo) | [Tabelog](https://tabelog.com/en/tokyo/A1301/A130101/13004097/) | Yes - required; Tue-Sat 5pm-10pm only | | Michelin 1-star since 2010. Chef Toshihiro Wada is a legend of the yakitori world. Only 16 counter seats -- intimate experience watching the master grill Okukuji Shamo free-range chicken over premium Bincho charcoal. The liver pate and wasabi-coated rare sasami are signature must-orders. "Juicy yet has bounce to the bite." Closed Sun-Mon. Multiple sources: Michelin Guide, Tabelog, Reddit, TripAdvisor, TimeOut. |
| Toriki (とり喜) | World's first Michelin-starred yakitori, Akadori chicken grilled over binchotan | Mid-range ~6,000-8,000/person | 1-8-13 Kinshi, Sumida-ku, Tokyo, Kosaka Bldg 1F (near Kinshicho Station) | [Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Toriki+Kinshicho+Tokyo) | [Tabelog](https://tabelog.com/en/tokyo/A1312/A131201/13026017/) | Recommended - weekends book up; try weekday after 9pm for walk-in luck | | First yakitori restaurant in the world to earn a Michelin star (2010), held it for 10 years. Counter-only seating. Chef grills Akadori chicken at incredibly high temperatures over binchotan charcoal, fanning with a large uchiwa fan -- mesmerizing to watch. The tsukune (ground chicken) and liver are standout items. Mon-Sat, closed Sun/holidays. Multiple sources: Michelin Guide, Tabelog, TimeOut Tokyo, Reddit. |

Only 9 seats around an open kitchen -- an intimate, once-in-a-lifetime experience. Smart casual dress code (no sandals/shorts/caps). Closed Sundays. For special occasions only. Multiple sources: Tabelog, TABLEALL, Pocket Concierge, TripAdvisor. |
